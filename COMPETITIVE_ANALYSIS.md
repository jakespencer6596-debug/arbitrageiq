# ArbitrageIQ Competitive Analysis
## Deep Research Report - April 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The sports arbitrage/+EV tool market is mature and crowded (OddsShopper, OddsJam, RebelBetting), with tools charging $100-250/month. However, **prediction market arbitrage is a wide-open gap**. No existing tool specializes in cross-platform prediction market arbitrage (Polymarket vs Kalshi vs PredictIt vs Betfair). This is ArbitrageIQ's unique opportunity.

**Key Finding: There is NO dedicated cross-platform prediction market arbitrage tool on the market.** The sports arb tools don't cover prediction markets. PredictionData.io provides the raw data API but no consumer-facing arb detection. Polymarket and Kalshi have zero cross-platform comparison tools built in.

## 7. OTHER PREDICTION MARKET ARB TOOLS (Market Gap Analysis)

### Existing Tools Found

**There are essentially NO dedicated cross-platform prediction market arbitrage tools.** Here is what exists in the adjacent space:

1. **Individual trader scripts/bots**: Various GitHub repos with Python scripts for pulling Polymarket/Kalshi data, but these are not products. They're one-off scripts by individual traders.

2. **Metaculus**: Community forecasting platform. Not a market (no real money). Useful as a "ground truth" signal but not an arb tool.

3. **Election Betting Odds (electionbettingodds.com)**: Aggregates prediction market probabilities for political events. Shows prices from multiple platforms side by side. But: read-only, no arb calculations, no alerts, limited to elections.

4. **Star Spangled Gamblers / Various substack newsletters**: Manual analysis of prediction market mispricing, but not automated tools.

5. **PredictionData.io**: As analyzed above -- B2B API, not a consumer tool.

6. **Custom Polymarket bots**: There's a community of automated traders on Polymarket using the CLOB API, but these are proprietary, not products.

### Why the Gap Exists
- Prediction markets are relatively new (Polymarket exploded in 2024)
- Cross-platform matching is technically hard (different event names, different contract structures)
- Volume/liquidity has only recently gotten large enough to make arbs worthwhile
- Regulatory uncertainty has kept traditional sports betting tool companies away
- The sports arb companies (OddsJam, OddsShopper, RebelBetting) see no reason to add prediction markets when sports is a $100B+ market

### THE MASSIVE OPPORTUNITY
**ArbitrageIQ is potentially the FIRST dedicated cross-platform prediction market arbitrage tool.** This is a genuine first-mover advantage. The market is ready:
- Polymarket volume: $500M+ on single markets
- Kalshi expanding rapidly into sports
- PredictIt still operational for politics
- Betfair covers some overlapping events
- FanDuel Predicts and DraftKings Predictions entering the space
- Platform-specific fees create consistent pricing inefficiencies

### What Professional Prediction Market Traders Actually Need
Based on how the sports arb tools are structured and what prediction market traders discuss:

1. **Speed**: Arbs disappear fast. Sub-5-second detection is table stakes for serious traders.
2. **Accuracy**: False positive arbs (caused by bad matching) destroy trust. Better to miss some than show fake ones.
3. **Fee awareness**: PredictIt's 10% profit fee + 5% withdrawal fee makes many "arbs" unprofitable. Must show net profit.
4. **Liquidity context**: A 5% arb on a $50 liquidity market is useless. Must show executable amount.
5. **Execution guidance**: "Buy YES on Polymarket at 0.36, Buy NO on Kalshi at 0.55" with direct links.
6. **Time value**: A 3% arb that locks capital for 6 months is bad. Annualized ROI is critical.
7. **Multi-outcome handling**: Many prediction markets have 5-20 outcomes. Overround detection matters.
8. **Alert fatigue management**: Don't spam with 0.5% arbs. Configurable thresholds.
9. **Bet tracking**: Log what you actually bet, track real P&L, compare to expected.
10. **Data edge**: External data (polls, weather forecasts, economic data) that suggests a market is mispriced BEFORE other traders notice.
